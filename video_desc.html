Description of the video

The video starts with a montage of major elections. It also shows speeches made by former presidents. It then shows a montage of people voting. During the montage, the narrator describes how the people are not actually voting for the President. After the montage, Professor William Ewald, Professor of Law and Philosophy of the University of Pennsylvania tells the viewers that the representation in the electoral college is equal to congressional representation. He also thinks that the designers of the system did not believe that it was the best system. The video now shows a graph indicating that the people are not voting for actual representatives, instead they are voting for a slate of electors. The video shows images of the American revolution, and images of the different branches of government. Professor Ewald tells the viewers that the system was very informal at the beginning. He also describes problems in American society at the time, which are illustrated by clips in the video. Professor Ewald then introduces the idea of a new constitution, but also describes the issues regarding the idea. The greatest issue concerned the leader of the country and their election. Different ideas were introduced like a committee elected by congress. The video shows some images illustrating this idea. Professor Ewald then tells the story of James Wilson, who introduced the idea of having one person in charge of the government. He wanted the people to directly elect this person. The video then shows images of the infrastructure of the age. The professor then tells the viewers about the concerns some people had regarding this system. Wilson then proposed an indirect election. The video now shows the constitution, and some illustrations about the negotiations. The viewers are then introduced to the chaos the elections brought. The video then shows some images that highlight the chaos (like people fighting). Political parties are then introduced to elections. The final solution to these issues was the winner take all system that sought to maximize the influence of individual states. The video ends with the credits and showing the National Geographic logo.
